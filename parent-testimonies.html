frequently asked questions

In a child-led learning environment, parents act as galvanizers, inspiring and motivating their children to learn independently. They provide a supportive atmosphere that fosters curiosity and self-discovery, without directly teaching or intervening in the learning process.

Parents can support this bold approach by fostering a growth mindset at home, encouraging their children to embrace challenges, and celebrating their efforts and progress. Open communication with teachers and active involvement in their child's education are also key.

As a parent, you can support your child's learning by inspiring them to learn on their own. Give them the freedom to explore and trust their ability to figure things out. Instead of providing direct answers, encourage them to think by asking questions.

It's important to avoid interrupting their thought process, explaining everything to them, rushing them for answers, or doing their work for them. Also, avoid comparing them with other children, as each child's learning journey is unique.

Encourage your child to work independently by giving them space and time to think and explore. Show extreme patience and trust in their self-learning abilities. Celebrate their unique learning journey without making comparisons with other children.

When your child makes mistakes, resist the urge to correct them immediately. Instead, give them time to think and encourage them to learn from their mistakes. This helps them develop problem-solving skills and resilience.

Asking questions is crucial as it stimulates your child's thinking and curiosity. It encourages them to seek answers on their own and fosters a deeper understanding of the subject matter.

Show your child that you trust their abilities and respect their learning pace. Provide encouragement and positive reinforcement, and celebrate their progress and achievements, no matter how small.
